Baked Ziti for Two (Mini Skillets)

If you think baked ziti is just a big batch situation, think again. These individual skillets are perfectly portioned and packed with everything you love about a classic baked ziti. Cheesy, saucy, with those golden, crispy tops that make it so good straight out of the oven.

Prep Time: 10 minutes

Cook Time: 40

Total Time: 50 minutes

Yields: 2


Ingredients

PASTA & SAUCE

  • 6 ounces ziti pasta
  • 1/2 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 tablespoon unsalted butter
  • 1/2 tablespoon finely minced garlic
  • 1/2 pound ground beef (90/10 or 85/15)
  • 3/4 teaspoon Italian seasoning
  • 3/4 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt, or to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 cups marinara sauce
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh basil
  • 2 tablespoons freshly grated parmesan

CHEESE LAYERS

  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella
  • 1/2 cup whole milk ricotta
  • 1/4 cup freshly grated parmesan


Instructions

COOK THE PASTA

  1. Bring a pot of salted water to a boil and cook the ziti until aldente.
  2. Drain and set aside.

MAKE THE MEAT SAUCE

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F.
  2. In a pan over medium heat, add olive oil and butter. Once melted, add the minced garlic and saute for a minute until fragrant and golden.
  3. Then add the ground beef and break it up as it cooks with a wooden spoon or spatula and season with salt and black pepper. Cook until browned, about 5–7 minutes, breaking it up as it cooks.
  4. Add the minced garlic, paprika, Italian seasoning, and red pepper flakes. Stir and cook for about 1 minute until fragrant.
  5. Pour in the marinara sauce and heavy cream, stirring to combine. Let it simmer for 3–5 minutes until slightly thickened.
  6. Stir in the chopped basil and parmesan, then add the cooked pasta and mix until everything is well coated.

ASSEMBLE THE SKILLETS

  1. In two mini oven-safe skillets, add enough of the pasta mixture to fill each skillet halfway.
  2. Add dollops of ricotta, then sprinkle mozzarella, then parmesan.
  3. Add more pasta on top.
  4. Finish with another layer of ricotta dollops, mozzarella, and parmesan.

Bake

  1. Place the skillets in the oven and bake for 10–15 minutes, until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  2. Broil for the last 1–2 minutes until the top is golden and slightly crispy.
  3. Let them sit for a few minutes before serving and enjoying!
